I moved to Singapore

2011-06-04

In 2007, I decided I wanted to live all around the world - to remove the separations of “us” and “them”.

Move to an opposite place and live there - immersed in local friends, language, culture, and daily life - until I really understand that point of view - until it feels like home. Then I’ll move to another opposite part of the world and do it again, until there are no more opposites left.

This will take 50 years, but I can do it, and it’s worth doing.

In 2009 I met Saj, and fell head-over-heels.

So on our second date I told her my plan and asked, “How would you feel about living in the rest of the world for the rest of your life?”

She’s an educational writer, writing English books for K-12 students around the world. She can do this from anywhere. So she thought a bit, and said, “Hmmmm... Yeah. I could do that.”

A year later, we got married. Two days after the wedding, we took one carry-on bag each, and went around the world, looking for a new country to call home.

We went down to Brazil, up to Sweden, around Europe, over to India and China. I kind of wanted to move to India, but she’s from there, so it failed the “opposite point of view” test for her. I kind of wanted to move to China, but she felt it was maybe too opposite for a first step. And then we found Singapore.

Singapore is 3 hours from India, 3 hours from China, on the border of Indonesia and Malaysia, so a wonderful hub for this entire part of the world.

On our first visit, we met so many amazing people from NUS, Unbox, Hackerspace, e27, EDB, and INSEAD.

Though there are 5 million people here, the music and entrepreneur scenes are quite small, so it feels like a small town in a big city, with the advantages of both. It’s a great place to set up a business, which is important to me. It’s an amazing travel hub, with cheap flights to 13 countries for under $200 - so it’s a great home base to get to know Asia.

I could go on and on about how much I love Singapore, but if you want a quick overview, the Discovery Channel did a great documentary about it, and the Wikipedia page is good.

We made some really dear friends on our first visit, and decided it was home. After 8 months of waiting for the paperwork to process, we became official residents of Singapore yesterday.

We rented an apartment. We got a kitten. I set up my new companies here. I’m an “entrepreneur in residence” at INSEAD, and mentoring at Beta. And I’m back to work.

So now you know where to find me. If you’re coming to Singapore, email me.
